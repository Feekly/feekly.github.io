Why Join?



Impactful Advocacy

Students can actively contribute to breaking the stigma surrounding menstrual health, promoting awareness, and advocating for accessible menstrual products. This involvement allows members to make a tangible difference in their campus community.

Skill Development

Participation in IRIZE provides opportunities for students to develop various skills, including communication, leadership, event planning, and community organizing. These skills are valuable both academically and professionally.

Community Engagement

Joining IRIZE connects students with like-minded individuals who share a passion for menstrual health and social justice. Through collaboration and teamwork, members can build meaningful relationships and create a supportive community on campus.

Personal Growth

Engaging in activism with IRIZE can foster personal growth by challenging students to step out of their comfort zones, confront societal taboos, and become more confident advocates for change.

Contribution to Campus Culture

By participating in IRIZE, students contribute to creating a more inclusive and supportive campus culture where menstrual health is openly discussed, and resources are readily available for all individuals.
